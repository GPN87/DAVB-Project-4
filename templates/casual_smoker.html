Below are some web-based smoking brief intervention tools to help you to assist with brief interventions on smoking in a primary healthcare setting

Brief Intervention tools for smoking cessation

  • Clinical Practice Guidelines for Smoking Cessation: These guidelines were developed by the Royal Australian College of General Practitioners and provide evidence-based recommendations for managing tobacco dependence in primary care settings. The guidelines include a section on brief interventions and provide guidance on how to deliver effective smoking cessation interventions in a primary care setting.
  • The 5As Framework: This is a web-based tool developed by the University of Ottawa that provides a framework for delivering brief smoking interventions in a primary care setting. The 5As Framework stands for "Ask, Advise, Assess, Assist, and Arrange follow-up" and provides guidance on how to deliver each step of the intervention.
  • Quitline Referral Platform This is a web-based tool that allows healthcare providers to refer their patients to state or provincial quitlines. The platform includes customizable referral forms, patient educational materials, and tracking tools to help providers monitor patient progress.
